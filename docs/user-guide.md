# FamilIA User Guide

## What FamilIA Is

FamilIA is designed to help older adults manage their finances more independently while keeping family members informed and maintaining a strong safety net around the experience. The site explains the product, lets people pick the right type of account, and shows both a tutor dashboard and a copilot flow that can request agentic responses from a remote endpoint.

## Typical Journey

### 1. Start on the home page

The home page explains what FamilIA does in simple terms:

- It helps spot suspicious bank activity.
- It turns bank information into clearer language.
- It sends timely alerts to family members.
- It keeps the older person involved instead of taking control away from them.

From there, you can move into signup or pricing.

### 2. Choose how you want to use it

On the signup and signin screens, FamilIA asks whether you are a family tutor or the older person being supported.

- Tutor access is for the family member who manages the setup and reviews alerts.
- Elder access is for the older adult who wants a simpler experience.

If you are the older person, the flow is intentionally lightweight and share-based so a family member can help you get started.

### 3. Review the dashboard

The dashboard is the main workspace for the tutor. It gives you a quick read on what is happening with the supported person:

- The overview shows recent activity and a wallet summary.
- The activity page lists past interactions and lets you filter by event type and severity.
- The finance page shows a clear view of balances, upcoming charges, and suspicious patterns.
- The settings page lets you update the elder profile, adjust the cash estimate, and manage the subscription.

### 4. Use the copilot assistant

The copilot page is the conversational part of the product. It can collect text, voice, and image input, send that information to a remote endpoint, and display the assistant's response back in the interface.

## Main Features

### Fraud protection

FamilIA highlights suspicious charges, strange withdrawals, and other signals that may need attention. The goal is to warn the family early, before a small issue becomes a bigger problem.

### Simple explanations

The app is built to make bank information easier to understand. Instead of depending on technical bank language, it presents summaries that are shorter and clearer.

### Shared care

The family can help without making the experience feel controlling. The tutor sees the important information, while the older person keeps the experience approachable.

### Activity history

The history page acts like a log of what happened. It helps you review consultations, emergencies, and document-related events in one place.

### Finance monitoring

The finance area brings together digital balance estimates, cash estimates, upcoming charges, and unusual transactions so the family can stay oriented.

### Assisted guidance

The copilot assistant turns the information you provide into a guided response. That makes it easier for the older adult to understand what is happening and what to do next.

### Settings and subscription

The settings page is where you keep the profile up to date, adjust the cash baseline, and review the current plan.

## What Each Page Is For

- Home page: learn what FamilIA does and how it helps.
- Pricing page: compare plans.
- Signup/signin pages: choose the right entry path.
- Dashboard overview: get a quick status summary.
- Activity page: inspect the event history.
- Finance page: review money-related information.
- Copilot page: submit text, voice, or image context and receive an assistant response.
- Settings page: update profile and billing details.

## Important Notes

- The dashboard uses mock data in development, while the copilot page sends live requests to the configured endpoint to obtain responses.
- Some settings are saved only in the browser on this machine.
- If you clear browser storage, profile settings will reset.

## Recommended First Actions

If you are new to the site, the simplest order is:

1. Read the home page.
2. Open signup or signin.
3. Enter the dashboard.
4. Check the activity and finance pages.
5. Update settings if needed.
